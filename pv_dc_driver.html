MPPT Heater Control

A PV panel behaves like a current source within a certain range. With excessive loading, voltage decreases and consequently power also decreases. With insufficient loading, voltage increases but current decreases, so power also decreases. Therefore, the load must be adjusted so that the maximum power of the panels is utilized at any given moment. We cannot regulate the heater resistance which is 'fixed', so we must regulate the current. Professional inverters use switching power supplies for this purpose, which allow voltage regulation on the load (thus also current regulation). Unfortunately, a power converter of about 2kW is too complex and the benefits of using it in our project are disproportionate to the time and effort. We will use PWM control.

Schematic diagram of heater control

  • PWM duty cycle: 70%
  • Panel current: 7A
  • Heater resistance: 17.63Ohm

The transistor switch will operate at 10kHz frequency, so by adjusting the duty cycle of the square wave we will be able to regulate the average current flowing[...] To prevent voltage changes on the PV panels from following PWM voltage changes, a capacitor bank must be used as an energy buffer. When the heater is off, energy from the panels will be stored in the capacitors (panels will charge the capacitors to their voltage). When the heater turns on, the capacitors will supply additional current and their voltage will not drop dramatically. In this way, we can regulate the average current of the load and the average voltage of the PV panels.

Through voltage and current measurements, we will learn the power at which the heater operates. To find the optimal operating point of the circuit, we will use an algorithm written in Python.

Voltage Measurement

ADC converter characteristic in ESP32

The ADC converter in ESP32 is not linear. Through experiments, it was found that 130mV should be added to the measurement result (operates in the range 130mV - 2500mV)
ADC converter calibration must be performed!!! Calibration instructions using the 'ESP32_ADC_calibration_tool'

Voltage measurement circuit on PV panels

  • Voltage measurement on the panel (buffer capacitor) using voltage divider (1:98.6)
    • Relatively precise voltage measurement in the range 13V - 250V (we assume maximum voltage 250V, while below 40V voltage we disable water heating)
    • Resistor values must be large so that large currents do not flow
    • R1=976kOhm, R2=10kOhm
    • For input voltage Uwe=250V, output voltage is Uwy=2.535V (according to simulation)
  • RAIL-TO-RAIL amplifier acting as voltage buffer
  • Low-pass RC filter with fg=33.86Hz (R=10kOhm, C=470nF)

Real-world tests

  • Current measurement using the ACS712 circuit works much better at 3kHz PWM frequency (instead of 10kHz)

  • RC filter (R=10kOhm, C=470nF) and ADC calibration work at f=3kHz

  • ACS712 tested on a breadboard is very sensitive to external interference (including touching it)

  • Measurement accuracy obtained (voltage) approx. 100mV (approx. 1A)

  • Building a prototype measurement circuit on a 10mOhm shunt is in progress. We will check how the circuit behaves and with what accuracy it measures the flowing current.
